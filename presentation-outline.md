# Presentation Outline

## Slide 1 - Title

Waste-Heat-Assisted Anaerobic Digestion for Decentralized Wastewater Treatment and Resource Recovery

## Slide 2 - Problem

- Urban wastewater treatment requires energy-intensive infrastructure.
- Anaerobic digestion produces useful biogas, but unstable temperature can reduce microbial efficiency.
- Low-grade urban waste heat is often released unused.

## Slide 3 - Proposed Idea

Use nearby waste-heat sources to maintain anaerobic digesters near 37 degrees Celsius, improving mesophilic microbial activity and supporting better resource recovery.

## Slide 4 - Biological Process

- Hydrolysis breaks complex organics into simpler compounds.
- Acidogenesis produces volatile fatty acids.
- Acetogenesis converts intermediates into acetate, carbon dioxide, and hydrogen.
- Methanogenesis produces methane-rich biogas.

## Slide 5 - Engineering Design

- Closed-loop heat exchanger captures low-grade heat.
- Digester operates in a controlled mesophilic range.
- Sensors monitor pH and volatile fatty acid risk.
- Inlet bypass protects the microbial system during toxic shock events.

## Slide 6 - Biogas Upgrading

- Hydrogen sulfide removal protects equipment and reduces corrosion.
- Carbon dioxide separation improves methane concentration.
- Upgraded biomethane can support local energy use after proper safety validation.

## Slide 7 - Optional PHB Loop

Separated carbon dioxide can be used as a carbon source for Cupriavidus necator under nutrient-limited conditions to produce PHB, a biodegradable polymer.

## Slide 8 - Expected Benefits

- Better temperature stability
- Improved methane generation potential
- Reduced unused urban heat
- Circular recovery of energy and materials
- Decentralized suitability for urban and peri-urban regions

## Slide 9 - Limitations

- This is a conceptual model, not a completed experimental system.
- Methane yield, PHB yield, and energy balance need lab or pilot-scale validation.
- Safety requirements for biomethane use must be reviewed before real-world deployment.

## Slide 10 - Conclusion

The proposed model links wastewater treatment, waste-heat recovery, biomethane upgrading, and optional bioplastic production into a practical circular bioeconomy framework.
