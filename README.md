## Project Summary

Municipal sewage sludge can be treated through anaerobic digestion to generate biogas. The efficiency of this process depends strongly on maintaining suitable microbial conditions, especially temperature and pH. This project proposes using low-grade waste heat from nearby urban sources, such as HVAC exhaust, industrial boilers, or data-center cooling loops, to maintain mesophilic reactor conditions near 37 degrees Celsius.

The model focuses on:

- Mesophilic anaerobic digestion for sludge stabilization and biogas production
- Closed-loop heat exchange using available urban waste heat
- Basic process monitoring using pH and volatile fatty acid indicators
- Biogas upgrading through hydrogen sulfide removal and carbon dioxide separation
- Optional carbon valorization using separated carbon dioxide for PHB production by Cupriavidus necator

## Revised Abstract

This conceptual study proposes a decentralized wastewater treatment model that improves anaerobic digestion efficiency by using low-grade urban waste heat to maintain mesophilic reactor conditions near 37 degrees Celsius. Municipal sewage sludge is processed through hydrolysis, acidogenesis, acetogenesis, and methanogenesis to generate biogas, while a closed-loop heat exchanger system recovers thermal energy from nearby sources such as HVAC exhaust, industrial boilers, or data-center cooling systems. Maintaining a stable mesophilic temperature may reduce hydraulic retention time, improve methane yield, and protect microbial activity during seasonal temperature fluctuations.

The model also includes basic process safeguards such as pH and volatile fatty acid monitoring to detect organic overloading or toxic shock events. Biogas upgrading through hydrogen sulfide removal and carbon dioxide separation is proposed to improve biomethane quality. As an optional carbon valorization pathway, separated carbon dioxide may be supplied to Cupriavidus necator under nutrient-limited conditions for polyhydroxybutyrate production. The proposed system highlights how wastewater treatment, waste-heat recovery, biomethane generation, and bioplastic production can be integrated into a circular bioeconomy framework suitable for urban and peri-urban settings.

## System Modules

1. Wastewater sludge input and pretreatment
2. Mesophilic anaerobic digester
3. Waste-heat capture and heat exchanger loop
4. Biogas cleaning and upgrading
5. Monitoring and safety bypass
6. Optional PHB bioplastic production loop
